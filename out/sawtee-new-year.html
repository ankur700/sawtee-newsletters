Dear friends and colleagues

As we near the end of the year, SAWTEE would like to reflect on the research and events that the organization has accomplished in the year 2079 B.S

As the year progressed, SAWTEE steadfastly upheld its commitment to its research, capacity building and advocacy activities on a diverse range of topics at national and regional levels. One of the major research outcomes of the year has been a study on the implications of Nepal’s expected graduation from the least developed country category three years hence. SAWTEE undertook studies on mainstreaming trade policy at subnational levels, economic diplomacy, value chain analysis of export commodities of interest to Koshi Province, gender dimensions of trade in Nepal, evaluation of innovation support programmes for small business, political economic analysis of Nepal’s public debt, among others.

SAWTEE organized in-person workshops, including a regional one. Most recently, it held a roundtable discussion to identify the required amendments to the Nepal-India Treaty of Trade, which is up for renewal in 2080 B.S., bringing together policymakers, the private sector, civil society and the media. In addition, SAWTEE organized a series of webinars in the second half of 2079 as part of a Civil Society Forum, on climate change, BIMSTEC Master Plan on transport connectivity, and intraregional investment in South Asia. We continued with the publication of Trade Insight, and issue briefs and policy briefs on varied topics.

We look forward to continuing our efforts in the coming year in the areas of research, advocacy and capacity building. If you have any feedback, please reach out to us through sawtee@sawtee.org or our social media accounts.

Thank you for your continued support. We wish you a happy, healthy, and prosperous New Year 2080 B.S.
